Montessori Daycare Board Member Self-Study

A guided, interactive learning portal to help board members understand Montessori principles, governance responsibilities, and the unique context of early learning in Vancouver.

Learning Pathway

This self-study experience blends Montessori pedagogy, strategic oversight, and community stewardship. Each module includes reflection prompts, resources, and action checklists.

Focus: Montessori philosophy, child development, and trauma-informed care.

  • Prepared environment and multi-age learning
  • Observation, independence, and peace education
  • BC Early Learning Framework alignment

Focus: governance, compliance, and risk stewardship.

  • Licensing, health, and safety requirements
  • Financial oversight and resource allocation
  • Board roles and decision-making cadence

Focus: partnerships, equity, and advocacy in Vancouver.

  • Relationships with families and educators
  • Indigenous reconciliation commitments
  • Community partnerships and local grants

Vancouver Context Snapshot

  • Licensing: Community Care and Assisted Living Act requirements apply to daycare operations.
  • Land acknowledgment: Vancouver is on the traditional territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh Nations.
  • Key funding: Child Care Fee Reduction Initiative and Affordable Child Care Benefit.
